Don’t just create a change. Be One!

  1. Home
  2. Blog Post

Posted on October 15, 2013 at 10:00 AM by Abhijith Asok

With the world advancing on a technological and commercial forefront at a lightning pace, the expectations and dreams of humanity have skyrocketed to an altogether different level. It just is not possible for someone to think small anymore, with eye-sparkling visions and zenith-grazing ideas. But, what exists remains in equilibrium, and hence, when one side goes up, the other sadly goes down. When visions flap their wings and flutter in the air, sustainability is towed down, deeper and deeper into soil. When dreams get wider, the actual realities get narrower. When development is on the rise, society is on an imbalance.

But, it would be a closed mindset of thought to say that the hope is entirely lost. Non – Governmental Organizations(NGOs) are those groups, with a dedicated passion associated with them to do good for the society and make sure its fabric stays intact, in the midst of the mad race for advancement. India alone has around 3 million NGOs who work for a variety of causes, groups and geographies. Help is always on the scene for various social causes through such NGOs who work to pull the society up from the puddle. But, there is always a ‘but’. How many of these NGOs are working to their full potential and smoothly continuing on, without any issues in their workflow? You would be surprised to see the quite meagre number, which is ironic to the fact that such a huge number of them actually exist. So what hinders these harbingers of goodwill from giving their best? What stands as an obstacle to their vocal chords, not allowing them to voice out as they want to? One Simple Word. A million reverberations – RESOURCES.

NGOs are non-profit organizations and hence, unlike any commercial or corporate enterprise, monetary gains aren’t their primary focus or not a focus at all. Therefore, business is clearly not their option of getting the finances they require for their projects, and they have a hard time seeking elsewhere to raise enough funds to put their plans into action. Yet, even if they manage to reach that destination somehow, all is not fine. Human resources would be the next stumble in front, where they do not have enough people to carry out what they want to. Their plans of a pan-country or a pan-state or even a pan-district project would require adequate human force, which would not be available always. Today’s scenario and societal outlook where anything social and non-profit is ‘generally’ looked down upon as a second-grade waste of time, is utterly disheartening and adding to the difficulties of NGOs even more. This setting needs a transformation. This landscape needs a difference. This panorama needs a CHANGE. This, is where ‘Changemaker.be’ comes to play.

We at ‘Changemaker.be’ are a group of value-driven youngsters who are creating a platform to bring the change-makers of the society together, namely Corporates, NGOs and Volunteers . We aim at the overall development of our society right from the grassroot level with effective cohesive mutually beneficial collaborations between these three sections of change-makers. Fund-seeking NGOs have a direct trail ahead in ‘Changemaker.be’ to attain their goal through the funding provided by the many corporates on board, depending on the projects, while the socially active and responsive corporates can effectively put their ‘Corporate Social Responsibility’ into direct action, by choosing the right NGO with the right project to suit their need, based on our suggestions or their own intuition or analysis, which would keep up with the expectations. Our platform will serve as a successful channel through which you can carry out your CSR where you can meet the people on the other end, ideate, plan out and execute, based on our suggestions and statistics, or on your intuition and judgement.’Changemaker.be’s volunteer database is the NGO’s reservoir for all the human resource they need, in the geography and stream they seek, to carry out their activities. The volunteers, in return get to be associated with the cause they want to support and the NGO they want to work with, and gets to go home with a sense of having made her/his contribution to the society, in addition to the perks the NGO would provide them.

It is a fact that such collaborations do happen currently, although informally and scattered, but, in many cases, it either works out very ineffectively or doesn’t happen at all, while with the same effort and expenditure, there could have been a collaboration which was highly optimized and tailored to the needs of all the three parties, with the right people on the other side, ensuring the benefits expected of the same to happen completely. The helping hands lent out are always mutual and it is that power of a concerned gesture that Changemaker.be harnesses.

Be a part of Changemaker.be and hold hands to solidify our society’s ladder. Every act of goodwill is one worthy of praise, and every person being part of it, is one worthy of appreciation.
